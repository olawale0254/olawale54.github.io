Giving hope to newborns!

Ubenwa is saving newborn lives by enabling quick and cost-effective diagnosis of birth asphyxia from infant cry.

Overview

The Challenge

Birth Asphyxia is one of the top 3 causes of infant mortality in Africa, causing the death of about 1.2 million infants and severe life-long disabilities (such as cerebral palsy, deafness, and paralysis) to an equal number annually. "If newborns who have asphyxia can be detected early enough, we may be able to save their lives" - UN.

What we are doing

We are applying artificial intelligence to automate analysis of amplitude and frequency patterns in the cries of known asphxiating and normal infants. Our ultimate goal is a diagnostic tool (mobile app, wearable) which can detect breathing difficulties simply by 'listening' to the cry of a baby. Our current results show sensitivity of up to 91% and specificity of 88%.

What we have achieved

As a model, our innovation has been deployed to a mobile app - Ubenwa - which harnesses the speech processing capabilities of today’s mobile devices to analyse a newborn’s cry and provide a qualitative assessment of whether or not a newborn has or is at risk of asphyxia. Aside delivering results much quicker, Ubenwa is atleast 99% cheaper than clinical alternative.
